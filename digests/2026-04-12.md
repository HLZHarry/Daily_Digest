## 💡 Worth Your Time / 今日精读

**[Quoting Bryan Cantrill](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Apr/13/bryan-cantrill/#atom-everything)**

This one rewards a slow read. Cantrill's observation — that human "laziness" (the drive to abstract, simplify, and eliminate unnecessary work) is a feature that LLMs fundamentally lack — cuts against the standard "AI will write better code" narrative in a precise and technically grounded way. For an AI/ML engineer thinking about where LLM-generated systems accumulate hidden complexity and technical debt, this is the kind of first-principles provocation that shapes better design instincts.

这篇值得细读。Cantrill指出人类"懒惰"（即抽象、简化、消除冗余的动力）是LLM从根本上所缺乏的特质，这与主流的"AI将写出更好代码"叙事形成精准而有力的对照。对于思考LLM生成系统如何积累隐性复杂性和技术债务的AI/ML工程师而言，这类追溯设计本源的思考有助于培养更好的工程直觉。
